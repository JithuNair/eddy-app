## What is open

The **source code** of Eddy is open source. You are free to:

- Read, study, and learn from it
- Fork it and build your own app (with attribution)

- Use it as a reference or starting point for your own work

## What you may do with a fork

If you fork the Eddy source code, you may:

- Build and distribute your own app based on the code
- Study the architecture and contribute learnings back
- Use the code as a foundation for a different product with a different name and identity

You must:

- Include clear attribution to the original project and creator in your fork's documentation and any public-facing materials (see Apache 2.0 Section 4(c))
- Make clear that your fork is **not** the official Eddy app

---

## What you may not do

You may not:

- Use the Eddy name, swirl logo, or app icon for a redistributed or competing app without explicit written permission from Jithu J. Nair
- Present your fork as the official Eddy app or imply endorsement by the original creator
- Remove or obscure attribution to the original creator
- Use the Eddy brand in a way that is confusing, misleading, or implies affiliation with the official project
