Eddy

Privacy Policy

Eddy is a calm, private ADHD companion app. This policy explains what data is collected, how it is used, and your rights as a user.

1. What Eddy Collects

Eddy collects the minimum data necessary to function:

  • Journal content — text entries, photos, and voice notes you create. Stored locally on your device and, if you enable backup, in your personal Google Drive App Data folder (private and accessible only by Eddy).
  • Google account information — your Google email address, used solely to authenticate you with Google Drive for backup and restore. Eddy does not store or transmit your Google account credentials.
  • Biometric data — if you enable the journal lock, your device's biometric system (fingerprint or face) is used locally to authenticate. Biometric data is never read, stored, or transmitted by Eddy.
  • Camera and microphone — used only when you take a photo or record a voice note inside the Journal. Media is saved to your device and your Google Drive backup (if enabled).
  • Usage and crash data — anonymised app usage events (e.g. which screens are visited, session length) and crash reports are collected via Google Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics. This data does not include journal content, photos, or voice notes.

2. How Data Is Used

  • Journal content is used only to display your entries within the app.
  • Google Drive backup is used solely to restore your data on a new device or after reinstalling.
  • Usage analytics help us understand how people use Eddy so we can improve it.
  • Crash reports help us identify and fix bugs.

3. Data Sharing

Eddy does not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes.

The only third-party services Eddy uses are:

  • Google Drive (optional backup) — Google Privacy Policy
  • Firebase Analytics — anonymised usage data — Firebase Privacy
  • Firebase Crashlytics — crash reports — Firebase Privacy

4. Data Storage and Security

  • All journal data is stored locally on your device.
  • If Drive backup is enabled, data is stored in your own Google Drive App Data folder — visible only to you and accessible only by Eddy.
  • Eddy does not operate its own servers and does not store your data on any external server controlled by us.

5. Children's Privacy

Eddy is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

6. Your Rights

  • Delete your data — uninstalling Eddy removes all local data. To delete your Drive backup, go to Google Drive → Settings → Manage apps → Eddy → Delete hidden app data.
  • Revoke Drive access — go to myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps with account access → Eddy → Remove access.
  • Opt out of analytics — contact us at the email below and we will provide instructions.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The updated date at the bottom of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of Eddy after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
